ABSORPTION SPECTRUM

The absorption spectrum is a unique pattern of light absorption that can help identify different gemstones based on how they interact with light. When white light passes through a gemstone, certain wavelengths are absorbed while others are transmitted, creating distinctive spectral patterns.

Understanding Absorption Spectra

Each gemstone has a unique absorption spectrum due to its chemical composition and crystal structure. These spectra can be observed using a spectroscope, which is an essential tool in gemology for identifying and authenticating gemstones.

Key Features

  • Absorption Lines: Dark lines or bands in the spectrum indicating absorbed wavelengths
  • Transmission Windows: Areas where light passes through freely
  • Characteristic Patterns: Unique combinations of absorption lines specific to each gem type

Common Absorption Patterns

Different gems show distinct patterns:

  • Ruby: Strong absorption in green and violet regions
  • Emerald: Characteristic absorption lines in red and blue-violet
  • Sapphire: Variable patterns depending on color variety
  • Diamond: Typically shows no absorption spectrum in visible light

Applications in Gemology

Absorption spectroscopy is used for:

  • Gem Identification
  • Authentication of natural vs. synthetic stones
  • Detection of treatments and modifications
  • Quality assessment
