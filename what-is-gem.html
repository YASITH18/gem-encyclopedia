What is a GEM?

A gemstone or gem is a piece of mineral crystal which, in cut and polished form, is used to make jewelry or other adornments. Most gemstones are hard, but some soft minerals are used in jewelry because of their luster or other physical properties that have aesthetic value.

Characteristics of Gemstones

Gemstones are characterized by their beauty, durability, and rarity. The following properties are used to identify and evaluate gems:

  • Hardness (resistance to scratching)
  • Crystal structure
  • Refractive index
  • Specific gravity
  • Cleavage
  • Color and pleochroism

Formation Process

Gems form under various geological conditions:

  • Igneous processes
  • Metamorphic processes
  • Sedimentary processes
  • Hydrothermal processes
